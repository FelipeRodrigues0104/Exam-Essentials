2.4 Contribution & breakeven analysis

Contribution

  • The giving or supplying as a part or share of the total.
  • Contribution per unit = Price – Average Variable Cost

Profit formula

  • Profit = Quantity x Price - (Fixed Costs + (Average Variable Cost x Quantity))

Breakeven point

  • It is where profit = 0 meaning the revenue covers the costs.
  • Step by step to calculate and graph the breakeven chart:
      1. Write profit equation = 0 ; 0 = Quantity x Price - (Fixed Costs + (Average Variable Cost x Quantity))
      2. Rearrange equation to find the unknown Quantity (values will be given in question).
      3. After quantity value is found do Quantity x Price to find the revenue.
      4. Plot a graph with Quantity in the x axis and revenue in the y axis.
      5. Mark the breakeven point (point with the quantity and revenue that you calculated in step 2 and 3).
      6. Draw a line from 0,0 that passes through the breakeven point and continue it until the end of the graph.
      7. Draw the fixed cost curve (straight horizontal line with the value of fixed cost given in question).
      8. Draw total cost line starting at the fixed cost line and passing through the breakeven point and continues until the end of the chart.
      9. Write a title for the chart; e.g. breakeven analysis for ____ at date ______.
      10. Remember to label the axis of the graph.
      11. Remember to label each line of the graph and the breakeven point.

  • The difference between current/forecasted sales and the quantity of sales at breakeven point.
